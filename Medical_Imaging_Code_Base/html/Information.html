Lung Health

X-ray images are commonly used to diagnose all of the following, plus more.

  • COPD (chronic obstructive pulmonary disease)
  • Emphysema
  • Chronic bronchitis
  • Pulmonary fibrosis
  • Cancer
https://lunginstitute.com/blog/how-are-x-rays-used-for-lung-disease/

Lung disease increases your chances of getting lung cancer, which is the most deadly cancer for Americans. Lung cancer has a 5 year survival rate of just 19%.

How can I reduce my chances of getting lung disease?

  • Don’t smoke, avoid secondhand smoke
  • Avoid chemicals in your home and workplace
  • Minimize exposure to poor air pollution outdoors, check your city’s air quality index
  • Prevent infection using regular hygiene practices
  • Get regular exercise
https://www.lung.org/lung-health-diseases/wellness/protecting-your-lungs

Perceptual Learning

Humans are excellent at perceptual learning. Given appropriate experience, humans will tend to drastically improve in their ability to make sense of what they see, hear, feel, taste or smell. Extensive research has shown that practice yields highly specific perceptual learning of simple visual properties such as orientation and contrast. These studies show that practice on simple stimuli such as Gabor patches or moving dots lead to improved orientation, contrast, or motion sensitivity. Further studies have also shown that people can acquire expertise in complex domains, such as in chess and medical imaging.
